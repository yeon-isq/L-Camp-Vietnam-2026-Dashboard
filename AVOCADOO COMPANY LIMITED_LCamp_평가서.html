ESG 임팩트 및 롯데 협업을 통한 임팩트 창출
A skincare app isn't going to move the needle on something like carbon emissions, so we'll stick to what it can actually do. On the social side, a lot of beauty spending gets wasted on products that don't suit the buyer, which means wasted money and half-used bottles in the bin. If we match people to products that actually fit their skin, and stay neutral instead of pushing whatever's on promotion, they buy less but better. With LOTTE's catalogue and real traffic, a pilot could show whether mismatched purchases and returns actually drop. Also on access: decent skincare advice in Vietnam usually means paying for a consultation or going to a clinic. A free in-store advisor that works in someone's own language reaches people who'd otherwise skip it, including older shoppers and tourists. On governance, we handle skin scans carefully and don't hold onto data we don't need, we keep recommendations brand-neutral rather than pay-to-promote, and we're upfront that it isn't medical advice. A pilot with LOTTE would be a way to show an AI recommendation engine can be useful and transparent at the same time.
